\subsection{The bench at the fair budget}
\label{sec:fairbench}
The bookkeeping debt is paid here: Table~\ref{tab:fairbench} repeats the
equivalence comparison at the fair budget of Section~\ref{sec:fair}
(CONGA with $K{=}16$ precomputed paths) on the corrected bench, real Abilene
included, twenty seeds per topology. At $\delta=2$\,pp the verdict counts are
9/12/10 of fifteen against CONGA and 10/10/10 against DRILL for the three cores
respectively; Section~\ref{sec:margin-sensitivity} shows how these counts
tighten with the margin. Two readings stand out. The equivalences concentrate
where the attractor predicts; and the real Abilene reaches equivalence with
neither engineered router for any core---the saturation boundary, visible in
the raw rates themselves.
